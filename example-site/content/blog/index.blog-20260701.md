## New Ovens, Same Starter

We finally retired the two cranky electric decks that have been limping along
since 2011 and swapped in a proper stone-hearth deck oven — same wood-fired
soul as the original, just enough room to bake twice the loaves at once.

The good news: our sourdough starter (yes, the one older than Maren's kids)
didn't notice a thing. First bake out of the new oven had the same crackling
crust and open crumb we've been chasing for fifteen years. If anything, the
rye came out even better — more even heat means fewer scorched bottoms for
whoever's on the early shift.

Come say hello to the new setup. We're celebrating with a free slice of
whatever's cooling on the rack, while it lasts.
